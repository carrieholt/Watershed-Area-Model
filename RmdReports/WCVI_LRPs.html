WCVI LRPs

Carrie Holt

January 18, 2021

This report contains preliminary LRPs for WCVI Chinook. Only indicators stocks with average PNI > 0.5 were included in these preliminary analyses. Results and LRPs with all indicator stocks (enhanced and unenhanced) are included at the end. Non-enhanced indicator stocks are: Artlish, Bedwell/Ursus, Cayeghle,Kaouk, Marble, Megin, Moyeha, Nahmint, Tahsis, and Tahsish. Enhanced indicators are: Burman, Conuma, Leiner, Nitinat, Sarita, Somass, Zeballos, San Juan, Tranquil, and Gold. Note, Tranquil is considered enhanced although PNI data are not avialble for this stock, and has been moved. Also, Gold have been moved to the enhanced category. Although a large portion of the fish in this system are not marked (PNI =0.52), most of the unmarked spawners are thought to be second generation (or descendents of) hatchery-origin fish from Robertson Creek hatchery. There is no evidence of the original natural-spawners in this system.

Figure 1. Time series of spawner abundances by inlet for indicator WCVI Chinook stocks with average PNI >0.5. Three reference points are included: SREP from the watershed-area model (horizontal green line) with 95% prediction intervals (green shaded area), and SMSY (horizontal yellow line) and SGEN (horizontal blue line) from watershed-area SREP values and expert-derived estimates of productivity.

For each year, CU-level status is red when any one inlet within the CU is below SGEN (lower benchmark) in that year. If all inlets within a CU are above their lower benchmark, then status is amber or green.

Figure 2. Annual CU Status, where 0 is red status and 1 is amber or green status

SMU-level status is identified from the proportion of CUs that are in the red zone. Only years when status of all three CUs are available are plotted and used in the logistic regression to derive an LRP (n=13).

Figure 3. Time-series of the proportion of CUs within the SMU that are in the red zone

The LRP is derived from a logistic regression of aggregate abundances to the SMU (summation of indicator stocks with average PNI > 0.5) versus the proportion of CUs above the red zone. A penalty is added to the estimation procedure to ensure the y-intercept of the curve is near zero. A provisional LRP at 95% of CUs above their lower benchmark is plotted, but alternatives can be considered (e.g., 2/3 of CUs). This logistic regression has a relatively poor fit to the data; in one year aggregate abundances are > LRP when only 1/3 CUs > red zone, and in 2 years aggregate abundances are < LRP but all CUs are > red zone. Diagnostic statistics and plots are in development.

Figure 4. Logistic regression of aggregate abundances vs proportion of CUs above the red zone, with an LRP at 95% of CUs above red zone (vertical dashed line, +/- 95% CIs = vertical dotted lines). Grey band are the confidence intervals for the fitted proportions of CUs.

Aggregate SMU abundances vary around the LRP, but are mostly below it. Note, the 95% confidence interval for LRP do not yet include uncertainty from the watershed-area regression. Full uncertainties that capture variabilty from benchamrks estimation will be larger.

Figure 5. Time-series of SMU abundances (summation of indicators stocks with average NI >0.5) relative to LRP.
